Ways Judgers and Perceivers Can Interact Better

If you are a JUDGER working with another JUDGER

·         Give in now and then, even when you know you are right.

·         Try to raise a question in place of giving an answer or offering advice.

·         Remember that some things are beyond the other's and your own control.

If you are a JUDGER working with a PERCEIVER

·         Avoid putting your colleague in a corner, conversationally. Allow room for them to move.

·         Help your colleague sort out what they do not want, knowing that you can live with the rest.

·         Appreciate your colleague's ability to generate alternatives. Don’t be afraid to consider them.

If you are a PERCEIVER working with another PERCEIVER

·         Make sure that someone makes a decision and follows through with it.

·         Help each other focus the conversation. Try to monitor when someone strays off course.

·         Try whenever possible to finish--hey, is that a new car?--your sentences.

If you are a PERCEIVER working with a JUDGER

·         Help your team understand that they cannot control everything-especially you.

·         Help your team see more than just answers and directions in what you are saying.

·         Remember that what your team mates are saying may not be final, even if they say it is.
