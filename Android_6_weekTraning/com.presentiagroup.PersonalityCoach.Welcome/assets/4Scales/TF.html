Ways Thinkers and Feelers Can Interact Better

If you are a THINKER working with another THINKER

·         Remember that each of you has emotions.  Be sure to consider feelings in your communications.

·         Be careful not to become too competitive in your conversations.

·         If others are present, be aware of how they will hear what you are saying.

If you are a THINKER working with a FEELER

·         Try to say “I’m sorry” once in a while, if only on general principle.

·         Say “I appreciate you”-whatever that means to you-with no purpose or goal in mind. Or, if you prefer, send a card.

·         Keep in mind that when your colleague surfaces an issue, he or she may want support, not analysis.

If you are a FEELER working with another FEELER

·         Face the disagreeable. Remember that it cannot be avoided.

·         Try not to rescue or personalize what a colleague may say.

·         Enjoy the affirmations and good feelings you give each other.

If you are a FEELER working with a THINKER

·         Don’t be afraid to have a knock-down-drag-out fight now and then.

·         Once in a while, try to express something negative without feeling guilty.

·         Avoid saying “I’m sorry” or “It’s my fault” to your colleague during an argument.
