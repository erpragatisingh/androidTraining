Introverted - Sensing - Feeling - Perceiving

 

Though they struggle constantly to maintain visibility, there is in the ISFP a love and sensitivity for others, as well as serenity and appreciation for life. The combination of Introversion, Sensing, Feeling, and Perceiving puts ISFPs more in touch with both themselves and the world around them than any other type. They have a very low need to lead and control others, and yet driven by a desire to see everything---plants, animals, and people---living harmoniously. ISFPs are not invaders of any living creature’s space but instead want to relate to and encourage all life to fulfill its potential. As a result of being so much in tune with and respectful of the natural boundaries of life around them, it can become difficult for ISFPs to understand the need of some people to impose limits or structure on others.

 

At Work

This type, often creative, artsy, and skilled in a variety of practical disciplines where people and nature are served, tends to be shy about offering his or her services, depriving the world of their contributions as a result. The term “ISFP manager” is almost an oxymoron. Though a significant part of the workforce, they are rarely positioned in leadership spots and would much prefer service-related positions. Indeed, they are not above passing up promotion so that they can stay “where the action is.” It’s not that ISFPs aren’t capable, although they do tend to be self-effacing. Indeed, they possess some impressive natural skills for relating to all forms of life---plants, animals, and people

 

As a result, it is this type more than any of the others whose style it is to stand alongside of another person (or plant or animal), with no intention to influence it, criticize it, or change it---perhaps not even interact with it---only to be in its presence. “Life and let live” might be the motto of the ISFP.

 

In Relationships

You can generally count on them for a listening ear, a tender touch, or a brief supportive word. Their quiet, unassuming posture lends itself to being there for those who need them. Those who require continuous verbal feedback or affirmations may find this type frustrating, but for the ISFP, the kindly deed speaks for itself.

 

Intimacy

It is a here-and-now event, experienced at a very deep level, with little or no need to talk or say anything about it to anyone -- including the ISFP's partner. Other tangible experiences, from cards and flowers to a gentle touch, may say it better than words.

 

Parenting

They have low needs to control, though usually aware of a great deal of the family's dynamics. They tend to express what is hoped for in the children. But beyond some gentle support, they let the rest of the process take its own course.

 

Conflict

Without a great deal of support and encouragement, conflict is about the worst thing in the world with which an ISFP must deal. Anything is preferred to out-and-out disagreement. When finally pushed beyond their limits, ISFPs can become tenacious, and it can seem totally disproportionate to their regular demeanor.
