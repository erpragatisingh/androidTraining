Introverted - iNtuitive - Thinking - Judging

 

INTJs view the world in terms of endless possibilities (iNtuition), to be manipulated, conceptualized, systematized, and translated through objective decisions (Thinking). These decisions are readily implemented because of their daily lifestyle of structure, schedule, and order (Judging). Their Introversion is the arena for developing their many ideas, which are usually many more than will ever be realized. However, the four preferences combine to convey confidence, stability, competence, intellectual insight, and self-assurance.

 

INTJs have an inner world rich with endless possibilities that, when combined with their Thinking-Judging preferences, gives them a drive toward improvement of everything. Indeed, these are the “better idea” people of the typological world. Everything—words, plans, designs, ideas, even people—has room for improvement. In the INTJ’s eyes, even the best can be made better.

 

At Work

When you consider how few INTJs there are in the U.S. population, it is astounding how much influence on corporate and academic life they have had. Their capacity for intellectual and conceptual clarity gives INTJs both vision and the will to see it through to completion---prized leadership qualities.

 

There are few things that a well-integrated INTJ cannot conquer with ease and grace. With their natural propensity for organization, these people are frequently elevated quickly in corporate circles and are looked to for exceptional leadership. They tend to be “big picture” people (iNtuitive) who can see the forest as well as the intricacies of its component parts. (To an INTJ, there’s more to a forest than merely trees.) With their capacity for follow through (Judging), they have a high completion rate in their many undertakings. People naturally look to them for a job well done, a word aptly spoken, an opportunity seized. And they usually rise to such occasions with ease.

 

In Relationships

Among the most visionary of the 16 types, whose gift to the world is independence. They prize their independence, and wish it upon everyone in their lives -- mate, children, friends, and associates. They face a constant challenge of reconciling the vision of a perfect relationship with reality. They are inclined to constantly improve every part of a relationship, not matter how good it may already be.

 

Intimacy

Like much of life for INTJs, intimacy can be richer in its imagined state than in its actual experience. They need to think about intimacy, frame it, understand it, then work constantly at improving it and deepening it.

 

Parenting

The greatest gift one can give a child as a parent is independence. To think independently, to stand on one's own two feet, to be your own person, is the essence of bringing up a child. Not seen as a warm, snugly nurturer.

 

Conflict

They typically respond to conflict with something like, "I'll have to think about that." Though they do not shy away from a stressful encounter, they believe that if we better understood the words we speak, any conflict could easily be transformed into a learning experience with a positive outcome. They are often blamed by a more sensitive mate for intellectualizing or overanalyzing a situation when the mate only wanted support and understanding.
